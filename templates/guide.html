1. Design of Experiments (DoE) PLANNING

Use this module when starting from scratch to create a statistically sound plan for your first experiments.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Define Features Go to the Features tab. Add your input variables. Choose Continuous for numeric ranges (e.g., Temperature 20-100°C) or Categorical for discrete options (e.g., Catalyst A, B, C).
  2. Step 2: Add Objectives Switch to the Objectives tab. Enter the names of the metrics you plan to measure (e.g., Yield, Purity, Cost). You don't need to specify targets yet.
  3. Step 3: Configure Tweaks In the DoE Tweaks tab, choose your design model. Box-Behnken is great for secondary surfaces, while Definitive Screening is efficient for many variables. Set your preferred number of experiments.
  4. Step 4: Generate & Export Click "Create table of experiments". Review the suggested matrix and click "Export CSV". You can now perform these experiments in your lab.

2. Bayesian Optimization (BO) OPTIMIZATION

The "brain" of the app. It analyzes your current data and recommends the next best settings to reach your goals.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Load your Data Drag and drop your experimental CSV into the upload zone. Ensure your results are filled in the spreadsheet before uploading.
  2. Step 2: Configure Goals In the Objectives tab, tell the AI what to do for each column: Maximize, Minimize, or hit a specific Target. Click the "Include" checkboxes for the goals you want to focus on now.
  3. Step 3: Run the Optimizer Click "Run Bayesian Optimization". The AI will use GPU acceleration (if available) to simulate thousands of possibilities and pick the top recommendations.
  4. Step 4: Review Pareto Trade-offs Look at the Pareto Front chart. Orange markers show experiments that achieve the best balance of all your objectives simultaneously.
Tip: If you have 4 or more objectives, the Pareto chart will automatically switch to a Table View so you can clearly see the optimal settings for high-dimensional problems.

3. Statistical Analysis (SA) INSIGHTS

Go beyond optimization to understand why your experiments are behaving the way they do.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Select Features & Targets Load your data, then go to the SA module. Check the boxes for the features you want to analyze and the objectives you want to predict.
  2. Step 2: Choose your Model In SA Tweaks, select Linear Regression for simple trends, or Neural Network/MLP if you suspect complex, non-linear interactions.
  3. Step 3: Run Analysis Click "Run the Statistical Analysis".

Understanding the Results

  • Correlation Matrix: A heatmap showing how strongly each numeric feature affects your goals. Blue is positive correlation; Red is negative.
  • Categorical Interactions: Interaction matrices showing how different combinations of levels (e.g., specific catalysts at specific pressures) impact the success score.
  • Feature Impact: Bar charts showing the relative importance of each variable for every specific objective.
  • Estimator Tool: Use the sliders at the bottom to predict What-If scenarios. The tool gives you a predicted value and a "Success Score" based on your defined targets.

4. Data Management WORKFLOW

How to effectively grow your dataset and keep your work organized.

The Commitment Flow

  1. When the BO module suggests experiments, they appear in a green-highlighted table.
  2. Perform those experiments and type the outcomes directly into the cells in that table.
  3. Once all result boxes are filled with numbers, click "Add all to Main Dataset".
  4. This merges your new data with the old, and instantly refreshes all plots.
